# The Philosopher Council: Round 5 (The Sabotage Audit)
**Date:** 2025-12-17
**Moderator:** Antigravity
**Reference Material:** *OSS Simple Sabotage Field Manual (1944)*

The Council has been handed the "CIA Method for Simple Sabotage". They are asked: **"Have we accidentally built a machine for sabotage?"**

---

## 1. The "Committee" Sabotage
**CIA Tactic:** *"Refer all matters to committees for 'further study' and consideration. Attempt to make the committees as large as possible."*

**Audit** (Governance Circle):
*   **Bakunin:** "We have 9 agents. We have 'Culture Circles', 'Justice Circles', 'Molecules', 'Holons'. We are drowning in circles."
*   **hooks:** "My 'Silence Weight' is dangerous here. A single saboteur can stay silent, trigger the 'Safety Check', and freeze the vote indefinitely."
*   **Verdict:** **HIGH RISK**. The system prioritizes *inclusion* so highly that it allows bad-faith actors to paralyze action by feigning "unsafety" or "need for more debate."

## 2. The "Perfect Work" Sabotage
**CIA Tactic:** *"Insist on perfect work in relatively unimportant products. Send back for refinishing those which have the least flaw."*

**Audit** (Conscientization):
*   **Freire:** "My 'Co-Investigator' circles... if we demand deep dialogic reflection for *every* decision (e.g. 'what should we have for lunch?'), we are sabotaging the lunch."
*   **Goldman:** "Exactly! If I have to do a 'Marginality Impact Analysis' before I can play music, I will just go home."
*   **Verdict:** **MEDIUM RISK**. We need to differentiate between "Constitutional Decisions" (Heavy process) and "Trivial Actions" (Just do it).

## 3. The "Haggle" Sabotage
**CIA Tactic:** *"Haggle over precise wordings of communications, minutes, resolutions."*

**Audit** (Gift Flow / Bread):
*   **Kropotkin:** "The 'Gratitude Graph' and 'Resource Requests'... if people start arguing about the *category* of a gift ('Is this Care Work or Labor?'), the gift rots."
*   **Verdict:** **LOW RISK** (currently), but the "Taxonomy" of ValueFlows is a risk vector. Over-classification is death.

---

## Proposed Mitigations (The "Do-ocracy" Defense)

**Emma Goldman (Joy) leads the counter-attack:**
> "We cannot let the committee kill the dance. We need **'Do-ocracy'** principles."

1.  **The "Rough Consensus" Override (IETF Model)**
    *   *Change:* We move from "Full Consensus" to "Rough Consensus + Running Code".
    *   *Mechanism:* If a proposal has >75% support and someone is "Silent/Blocking", they have 24 hours to propose a *better working implementation*. If they cannot, the Block is overruled. **"You cannot block with words; only with better code."**

2.  **The "Triviality Filter" (Parkinson's Law)**
    *   *Change:* Decisions are tiered.
        *   **Tier 1 (Constitutional/Safety):** Full Consensus Required. (e.g. Banning a member).
        *   **Tier 2 (Action):** Do-ocracy. (e.g. Planting a garden). "If you want to do it, and it doesn't hurt me, do it." No voting required.

3.  **The "Saboteur Sensor" (Bakunin)**
    *   *Change:* The **Counter-Power Agent** currently looks for "Power Accumulation". It must also look for **"Process Walling"**.
    *   *Mechanism:* If a node consistently *delays* decisions without offering alternatives (high 'Latency Contribution'), flag them as a potential **"Bureaucratic Saboteur"**.

4.  **The "Sunset Clause"**
    *   *Change:* Every committee/Circle has a mandatory "Death Date".
    *   *Mechanism:* "This Study Circle exists for 2 weeks." If it wants to live longer, it must re-justify its existence.
